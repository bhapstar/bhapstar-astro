How the Timing signal works

Turn on Timing, then type into the currency you’re sending from. Each other row shows where today’s rate sits versus its own recent range. Tap a question below.

Whether today is a relatively good or poor moment to convert — compared with the past, not the future. Favourable means you’re getting more of the target currency per unit sent than on most recent days.
▲ favourable top third of the window’s range.
◆ around avg middle of the range.
▼ below avg bottom third — weaker than usual.
◆ pegged / flat the pair barely moves.
They set how far back today is compared against. 90D answers “better than the last few months?”; 10Y shows where today sits across a decade. Longer windows give more context but react more slowly.

The dirham is fixed to the US dollar (~3.6725), so AED↔USD barely moves. But AED↔GBP and AED↔INR do move, because the pound and rupee float against the dollar — that’s where the signal matters for you.
Live conversion uses a real-time mid-market feed. Timing history uses a free public dataset built on European Central Bank rates, going back to 1999, anchored to the US dollar (with pegged currencies like AED derived from their fixed rate). Cached for ~12 hours.
Major floating currencies (GBP, EUR, INR, CAD, JPY, AUD, CHF and more) plus USD-pegged ones including AED, SAR, QAR, OMR and BHD. A few thinly-traded currencies may show “no history” — live conversion still works for all of them.
